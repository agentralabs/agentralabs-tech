# Initial Problem Coverage

This document captures the initial problem set that motivated the creation of AgenticCognition and maps each problem to its solution.

## Problem: AI forgets who you are between sessions

Solved by persistent `.acog` files that preserve the living user model -- beliefs, values, patterns, shadows -- surviving restarts, model switches, and long gaps.

## Problem: AI responds to words but misses patterns

Solved by decision fingerprinting and belief graph analysis that detect recurring patterns, biases, and cognitive blind spots.

## Problem: Beliefs and values drift silently over time

Solved by drift tracking with value tectonics that monitors how convictions shift, harden, or erode session over session.

## Problem: Unconscious biases and shadow beliefs are invisible

Solved by shadow psychology that maps projections, defended regions, emotional triggers, and blindspots the user cannot see.

## Problem: AI cannot predict how you would react to something new

Solved by the preference oracle and decision simulation that use the full belief graph to predict reactions before they happen.

## Problem: No archaeological record of how thinking evolved

Solved by reasoning fossils, cognitive strata, and growth rings that preserve the full history of who the user was at every stage.
